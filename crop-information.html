Cereal Crops

Wheat (Triticum spp.)

Wheat is one of the world's most important cereal crops, providing essential nutrients and calories to billions of people.

Best Sowing Practices

  • Sowing Time: October to December (Rabi season)
  • Seed Rate: 100-125 kg/hectare
  • Spacing: Row to row: 20-22.5 cm
  • Depth: 5-6 cm
  • Method: Line sowing with seed drill is recommended

Soil Requirements

  • Soil Type: Well-drained loamy to clay loam soils
  • pH Range: 6.0-7.5
  • Drainage: Good drainage is essential
  • Land Preparation: Fine tilth with 2-3 ploughings

Pest and Disease Control

Common Pests:
  • Aphids: Spray Imidacloprid 17.8 SL @ 100 ml/ha
  • Termites: Apply Chlorpyriphos 20 EC @ 3.5-4 liters/ha
Common Diseases:
  • Rust: Spray Propiconazole 25 EC @ 500 ml/ha
  • Powdery Mildew: Spray Hexaconazole 5 EC @ 500 ml/ha
  • Loose Smut: Seed treatment with Carboxin 37.5% + Thiram 37.5% @ 2g/kg seed

Fertilizer Recommendations

  • Nitrogen (N): 120 kg/ha (50% as basal, 25% at tillering, 25% at flowering)
  • Phosphorus (P): 60 kg/ha (as basal application)
  • Potassium (K): 40 kg/ha (as basal application)
  • Zinc: 25 kg/ha Zinc Sulfate (if soil is deficient)
  • Organic Manure: 10-15 tons/ha of well-decomposed FYM
