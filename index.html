Major Agricultural Challenges

Understanding the key challenges facing farmers in India today

Fragmented Land Holdings

Over 86% of farmers operate on less than 2 hectares of land, making mechanization and economies of scale difficult.

Water Scarcity

Despite being the largest extractor of groundwater globally, India faces severe depletion in states like Punjab and Haryana. Traditional irrigation methods waste water and leave tail-end farmers underserved.

Climate Change

Erratic monsoons, rising temperatures, and extreme weather events like droughts and floods reduce crop yields and soil fertility.

Inadequate Infrastructure

Poor storage and transportation lead to post-harvest losses of up to 10%. Many farmers rely on middlemen, reducing their profit margins.

Limited Access to Credit

Small farmers often lack collateral and face high-interest rates, pushing them into debt traps and distress sales.

Low Technological Adoption

Many farmers are unaware of or can't afford modern tools like precision farming, drip irrigation, or digital advisory platforms.

Policy Gaps

Minimum Support Price (MSP) schemes cover only a few crops and regions. Subsidies often don't reach the intended beneficiaries.

Environmental Degradation

Overuse of chemical fertilizers and pesticides has led to soil nutrient imbalance, water pollution, and biodiversity loss.
