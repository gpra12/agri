Eligibility Information

Land Ownership Requirements

  • Proof of land ownership or lease agreement
  • Land records verification through local revenue department
  • Minimum land holding requirements (scheme-specific)
  • Land classification (irrigated/non-irrigated) documentation

Income Criteria

  • Annual income limits (varies by scheme)
  • Income certificate from authorized government official
  • Tax returns for previous financial years
  • Bank statements showing agricultural income

Farmer Categories

  • Small & Marginal Farmers (less than 2 hectares)
  • Medium Farmers (2-10 hectares)
  • Large Farmers (more than 10 hectares)
  • Tenant farmers & sharecroppers (special provisions)

Special Categories

  • Women farmers (additional benefits)
  • Scheduled Caste/Scheduled Tribe farmers
  • Farmers in drought-prone or disaster-affected areas
  • First-time farmers & young agriculturists

Application Process

1

Scheme Selection

Identify the appropriate scheme based on your farming needs, land holding, and eligibility criteria. Consult with local agriculture department officials or our experts for guidance.

2

Document Preparation

Gather all required documents including identity proof, land records, bank details, income certificates, and scheme-specific documents. Ensure all documents are current and properly attested.

3

Application Submission

Submit your application through the appropriate channel - online portal, Common Service Center, Bank, or Agriculture Department office. Keep a copy of the application and acknowledgment receipt.

4

Verification Process

Field verification may be conducted by government officials to validate your application details. Be available during this process and provide any additional information if requested.

5

Approval & Disbursement

Upon approval, benefits will be disbursed directly to your bank account through Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT). Track your application status through the scheme portal or helpline numbers.

Government Schemes & Subsidies

From affordable loans to subsidies and storage assistance, here are some key government schemes that can benefit your farming operation:

Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN)

Income support scheme that provides direct financial assistance of ₹6,000 per year to eligible farmer families in three equal installments.

  • Eligibility: All landholding farmer families with cultivable land
  • Benefits: Direct income support of ₹6,000 per year
  • Application Process: Online registration through PM-KISAN portal or at Common Service Centers
Learn More

Kisan Credit Card (KCC) Scheme

Provides farmers with affordable credit for their agricultural needs, including crop production, post-harvest expenses, and farm maintenance.

  • Eligibility: All farmers, tenant farmers, sharecroppers, and self-help groups
  • Benefits: Flexible credit limits, low interest rates, and insurance coverage
  • Application Process: Apply at local banks, cooperative banks, or regional rural banks
Learn More

Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY)

Comprehensive crop insurance scheme that provides financial support to farmers in case of crop failure due to natural calamities, pests, or diseases.

  • Eligibility: All farmers growing notified crops
  • Benefits: Insurance coverage for the entire crop cycle at minimal premium rates
  • Application Process: Automatic enrollment for loanee farmers; non-loanee farmers can apply through insurance companies or banks
Learn More

Soil Health Card Scheme

Provides farmers with information on soil nutrient status and recommendations on appropriate dosage of nutrients for improving soil health and fertility.

  • Eligibility: All farmers
  • Benefits: Free soil testing and personalized recommendations for soil improvement
  • Application Process: Apply at local agriculture department offices or Krishi Vigyan Kendras
Learn More

National Agriculture Market (e-NAM)

Electronic trading platform that connects farmers directly to markets across the country, eliminating middlemen and ensuring better prices.

  • Eligibility: All farmers
  • Benefits: Better price discovery, reduced transaction costs, and access to a wider market
  • Application Process: Register at local APMC markets connected to e-NAM
Learn More
